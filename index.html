Ekaterina Vinnik

Russian, M.D., Ph.D. in cognitive neuroscience; discovered an auditory illusion. I love doing research as much as my practical work (one-to-one consultations). That plus exploring the world with my 3 year old son and hiking is what makes my heart beat.

André Maia Chagas

Andre is a researcher at the Ophthalmic research laboratory at the University of Tuebingen, and has been actively working with open source technologies for the democratization of science for over 7 years. He has started the openeuroscience.com portal and contributed with Trend in Africa (an NGO dedicated to science education) teaching researchers how to build their own lab equipment. More recently he has co-founded Prometheus Science a startup dedicated to making open source scientific hardware the norm rather than the exception in labs worldwide. In his talk we are going to cover the current affairs in open science and why adopting them benefits research at the individual as well as at the collective level.

Thiago Carvalho

I studied Biology at the University of Sao Paulo, Campinas. In 1998, I joined the Instituto Gulbenkian de Ciência in Lisbon to do my PhD in immunology. In 2003 I moved to the University of Alabama, Birmingham as an Irvington Institute fellow, working on B cells with John Kearney. Four years later, I returned to Portugal to be the associate curator for the Gulbenkian Museum's Darwin exhibition. Once the "Darwin's Evolution" show closed in 2009, I returned to the IGC as director of graduate programs, which included a period as interim director of the MD PhD program. In 2015 I moved to New York to take a position as senior scientific editor at the Journal of Experimental Medicine (Rockefeller University Press), where I remained a little over two years. This summer, I came to the CCU as Graduate Studies Coordinator.

Danbee Kim

Danbee (INDP 2013) spends a lot of time thinking about permaculture, anarchy, circus, and education. She currently studies cuttlefish, develops general principles of intelligence, and builds interactive art installations that collaborate with the public to measure human behavior and psychophysics.

Gonçalo Lopes

Gonçalo is a software engineer turned neuroscientist. He completed his PhD at the CCU with Adam Kampff and Joe Paton, trying to understand what is the role of motor cortex in the control of movement in non-primate mammals. Along the way, Gonçalo developed the open-source Bonsai visual programming language as a way to rapidly prototype interactive neuroscience experiments.

The Venue

Quinta dos Machados, Mafra

Set in a 15 hectare woodland property, this hacienda-style, refined hotel with an annex is 4.5 km from Tapada Nacional de Mafra park and 14 km from the Castro of Zambujal archaeological site.
Bright rooms with wood furnishings and en suite bathrooms feature TVs and free Wi-Fi. Upgraded rooms add tea and coffeemakers and forest views. The 2 independent houses include kitchens, living/dining areas and balconies.
Amenities include a plush restaurant, an exercise room, and a spa area with a Turkish bath and a sauna. There's also event space and an outdoor pool.
